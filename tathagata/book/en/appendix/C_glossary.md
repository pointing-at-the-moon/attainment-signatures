# Appendix C　Glossary

In the main text, every term was glossed in plain language at its first appearance; the Sanskrit romanization and diacritics are gathered here, so as not to disturb the text.

Each entry gives: **English term / Chinese — romanization (Skt) — a one-line gloss**. This being a Mahāyāna volume, the romanization is chiefly Sanskrit; terms native to the Chinese rendering, or established by later doctrinal classification, or the book's own reading, carry no romanization, marked instead (Ch.) (classification) (book's reading) (reserved to author).

---

## I. The Buddha-Fruit's Mastery and Its Frame

- **Mastery / freedom (自在)** — vaśitā / aiśvarya — unobstructedness, freedom; what the book reads is that buddha-fruit freedom in which "not one place still remains provisional."
- **The Tathāgata's mastery (如來自在)** — (the book's subject) — the buddha-fruit's mastery; its deepest mark, "name and reality coincide, not one place still provisional."
- **Unobstructed (無礙)** — apratihata — the book's keynote; not "unending (measureless, boundless)," but "not one hindrance remaining, not one thing left outside." A subtraction, not an addition.
- **The name's fullness is the mastery's fullness (名滿即在滿)** — (book's reading) — the name is full (the ten epithets complete) precisely because the reality is full (the mastery perfected); name and reality met in one place.
- **The fruit-level is not the causal-level brightened (果位非因位之調亮)** — (book's reading) — the buddha-fruit's virtues are not the causal virtues enlarged, but one and the same brought to fruit-level fullness.
- **The arhat's / bodhisattva's mastery (阿羅漢自在／菩薩自在)** — (the twin / first two volumes' subjects) — the mastery of going out (going, all suffering quieted) / the mastery of staying (not going, refusing quiescence, benefiting beings); with the Tathāgata's "mastery of the unobstructed," the arc of the attainment axis's three volumes.

## II. Name — The Ten Epithets

- **The ten epithets (十號)** — daśa-nāman — a Buddha's ten names; not ten titles affixed from without, but ten faces of one mastery.
- **Thus-Come (如來)** — tathāgata — come along the path of suchness; first of the ten.
- **Worthy-of-Offerings (應供)** — arhat — worthy to receive the offerings of gods and humans (foe-slayer, hence worthy).
- **Rightly-and-Fully-Awakened (正遍知／正等覺)** — samyaksaṃbuddha — rightly and pervadingly knowing all dharmas.
- **Perfect-in-Knowledge-and-Conduct (明行足)** — vidyācaraṇa-saṃpanna — the three knowledges (past-lives, divine-eye, outflow-exhaustion) and conduct complete; the two vehicles' three knowledges not full, a Buddha's full.
- **Well-Gone · Knower-of-the-World · Unsurpassed-One · Tamer-of-Persons · Teacher-of-Gods-and-Humans · Buddha · World-Honored One (善逝·世間解·無上士·調御丈夫·天人師·佛·世尊)** — sugata / lokavid / anuttara / puruṣadamyasārathi / śāstā devamanuṣyāṇām / buddha / bhagavat — the remaining epithets; Buddha = one awake and clearly knowing beneath the bodhi tree, World-Honored One = one possessed of virtue.

## III. Body — The Three Bodies

- **The three bodies (三身)** — trikāya — self-nature body (dharma-body) · enjoyment body (reward-body) · transformation body; not three Buddhas, but one mastery showing three faces by capacity.
- **Dharma-body / self-nature body (法身／自性身)** — dharmakāya / svābhāvika-kāya — a Buddha's markless substance, the basis on which all dharmas turn in mastery; the basis of the reward and transformation bodies.
- **Reward-body / enjoyment body (報身／受用身)** — saṃbhogakāya — the body that enjoys the dharma-bliss; self-enjoyment (all-pervading) · other-enjoyment (shown by capacity to ground-dwelling bodhisattvas).
- **Transformation body (化身／變化身)** — nirmāṇakāya — the body shown to deliver ordinary beings and the two vehicles.
- **Transformation of the basis (轉依)** — āśraya-parāvṛtti — turning the defiled into the pure, casting off the two hindrances; the three bodies are what this transformation discloses.
- **The three kinds of permanence (三種常)** — (Buddhabhūmi classification) — the dharma-body's permanence-of-nature, the reward-body's permanence-without-break, the transformation-body's permanence-in-continuity; "the three bodies are permanent" being a Yogācāra / *Sūtrālaṃkāra*-line classification.

## IV. Wisdom — The Four Wisdoms

- **The four wisdoms (四智)** — catur-jñāna — great-perfect-mirror, equality, wondrous-observing, duty-fulfilling; turned out of the eight consciousnesses, not spirit-cognitions acquired apart from severance.
- **Great-perfect-mirror wisdom (大圓鏡智)** — ādarśa-jñāna — turned from the eighth consciousness; like a great round mirror showing all images.
- **Equality wisdom (平等性智)** — samatā-jñāna — turned from the seventh; observing self and other all equal, ever joined with great compassion; establishes the buddha-ground's non-abiding nirvāṇa.
- **Wondrous-observing wisdom (妙觀察智)** — pratyavekṣaṇā-jñāna — turned from the sixth; severing all doubt, raining the great dharma-rain; opened toward beings, the four analytical knowledges.
- **Duty-fulfilling wisdom (成所作智)** — kṛtyānuṣṭhāna-jñāna — turned from the first five; manifesting transformations throughout the ten directions, accomplishing what the original vows would do.
- **Turning consciousness into wisdom (轉識成智)** — (expedient designation) — said, to urge reliance on wisdom and the letting-go of consciousness; wisdom is not consciousness, nor a thing apart from it.
- **Pure dharma-realm / the buddha-ground's five dharmas (清淨法界／佛地五法)** — (Ch.) — pure dharma-realm + four wisdoms = the buddha-ground's five dharmas; the five subsume the three bodies.

## V. Function — Powers, Fearlessnesses, Distinctive Dharmas, Analytical Knowledges

- **The ten powers (十力)** — daśa-bala — a Buddha's ten "powers of wisdom" (each a kind of "knowing-as-it-is," not spirit-power): the possible-and-impossible, karma-and-fruit, meditations, faculties, inclinations, dispositions, paths-and-where-they-lead, past lives, the divine eye, the exhaustion of the outflows.
- **The four fearlessnesses (四無所畏／四無畏)** — catur-vaiśāradya — a Buddha's four fearlessnesses before the assembly, with nothing to be challenged: all-knowledge, the exhaustion of outflows, declaring what obstructs the path, declaring the path that ends suffering. The first two self-benefit, the latter two other-benefit.
- **The lion's roar (師子吼)** — siṃha-nāda — a Buddha's fearless proclaiming before the great assembly, able to turn the Brahmā-wheel; an image of His teaching, beyond challenge.
- **The eighteen distinctive dharmas (十八不共法)** — aṣṭādaśa-āveṇika-buddha-dharma — eighteen virtues the buddha-ground alone possesses fully, in which the śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas have no share: three non-lapses, six non-diminutions, three actions following wisdom, the three-times knowledge unobstructed, and the rest.
- **The four analytical knowledges (四無礙解／四無礙智)** — catuḥ-pratisaṃvid — penetration without impediment in four respects: meaning, dharma, language, glad speaking; "unobstructed" = penetration without impediment, the wondrous-observing wisdom opened toward beings.
- **The Tathāgata's masterful function (如來自在用)** — (Avataṃsaka phrase) — the "Sage Leader" chapter's verse names the ten powers, fearlessnesses, and eighteen distinctive dharmas "the Tathāgata's masterful function, all manifested to deliver beings"; the eye of this book's Part Four.

## VI. The Marks, the Great Compassion, the All-Kinds Wisdom

- **The thirty-two marks and eighty signs / secondary marks (三十二相·八十種好／八十隨形好)** — dvātriṃśal-lakṣaṇa / aśīty-anuvyañjana — a Buddha's marks and signs; an adornment of the "born-body," felt as fruit of cultivated cause, shown for delivering beings, the seal "that can be seen" upon the form-body.
- **Born-body / dharma-body (in the marks' context) (生身／法身)** — (Ch.) — the born-body adorns itself with the marks and signs, the dharma-body by the virtues of wisdom; "for the born-body the thirty-two marks are spoken, for the dharma-body the markless."
- **Great loving-kindness and compassion (大慈大悲)** — mahāmaitrī-mahākaruṇā — great loving-kindness giving joy to all beings, great compassion drawing out their suffering; "the loving-kindness and compassion in the mind of the buddhas is named great" (arising from the buddha-mind, tied to the sequence of the eighteen distinctive dharmas, not an enlarging of the quantity of feeling).
- **The great compassion as root (大悲為根本)** — (Nirvāṇa phrase) — the buddhas of the three times have great compassion for their root, "were there no great compassion, He would not be called a Buddha"; the great compassion constitutes a Buddha, not an ornament to be added or subtracted. (Note: "compassion of one substance" (同體大悲) is a later expression, absent by the letter from the three sutras drawn on here, and not used in this book.)
- **The all-kinds wisdom (一切種智)** — sarvākāra-jñatā — prajñā's fruit-level fullness in the buddha-mind (observing the various dharma-gates, breaking ignorance); prajñā in the buddha-mind "changes its name" to the all-kinds wisdom—not an acquired omniscience.
- **The all-knowledge (一切智)** — sarvajñatā — the general mark, the cause (breaking ignorance in general); set against the all-kinds wisdom (the particular mark, the fruit).
- **Prajñā-pāramitā (般若波羅蜜)** — prajñā-pāramitā — the wisdom that reaches the far shore; the bodhisattva seeking to cross, named pāramitā; the Buddha having crossed, named the all-kinds wisdom (one and the same prajñā, the name differing at cause and fruit).

## VII. Perfection and Terminus

- **The Tathāgata's ten masteries / the buddhas' ten masterful dharmas (如來十自在／諸佛十種自在法)** — (Avataṃsaka · fruit level) — the buddhas' ten masterful dharmas, with "manifesting all buddhas in a single thought yet never using the least skillful effort," effortless spontaneity, for their aspect; listed in a separate chapter from the eighth-ground bodhisattva's ten masteries.
- **The eighth-ground ten masteries (八地十自在)** — (Avataṃsaka · causal level) — the immovable-ground bodhisattva's ten masteries of life, mind, wealth, action, birth, vow, understanding, as-wished, wisdom, dharma; causal level, a different register from the Tathāgata's ten.
- **Effortless spontaneity (任運無功用)** — anābhoga — self-operating without a hair's breadth of contrived force; the aspect of the buddha-fruit's ten masteries ("never using the least skillful effort").
- **Permanence-bliss-self-purity (the four virtues of the great nirvāṇa) (常樂我淨)** — nitya-sukha-ātma-śuddha — the great nirvāṇa's four virtues; the scripture judges the world's permanence-bliss-self-purity to have "words without meaning" (the four inversions), the supramundane great nirvāṇa's to have "words with meaning," matched to the Tathāgata's dharma-body (permanence) · nirvāṇa (bliss) · the Buddha / tathāgatagarbha (self) · the true dharma (purity). **The definitive sense of its "self" virtue this book records from scripture and does not adjudicate, leaving the telling to the author (see "reserved to the author," below).**
- **Tathāgatagarbha / buddha-nature (如來藏／佛性)** — tathāgatagarbha / buddhadhātu — the buddha-nature all beings inherently have; nirvāṇa's "self" virtue points, on the face of the words, to the tathāgatagarbha-buddha-nature, not the ordinary being's five-aggregate false self. Its definitive sense is reserved to the author.
- **The ultimate non-abiding nirvāṇa (究竟無住處涅槃)** — apratiṣṭhita-nirvāṇa — suchness emerged from the cognitive hindrance, ever supported by great compassion and prajñā, not abiding in birth-and-death or nirvāṇa, benefiting beings to the end of the future, functioning yet ever-quiescent; the buddha-fruit's terminus, "mastery as non-abiding," not cessation.
- **The unobstructed liberation (無礙解脫)** — apratihata-vimokṣa (traditional restoration) — the afflictions and traces pulled out at the root, unobstructed in wisdom over all dharmas, the liberation truly indestructible; the book's keynote "unobstructed" come to its name. (Note: the precise analysis of the two Sanskrit words a-pratiṣṭhita "non-abiding" of the nirvāṇa and a-pratihata "unobstructed" of the keynote is left to the author; in Chinese the book takes only "non-abiding is the nirvāṇa-face of unobstructedness.")
- **The vajra-like samādhi (金剛喻定)** — vajropama-samādhi — the concentration of the threshold thought-moment of becoming a Buddha; in a single thought-moment the two hindrances are cut at once, the tathāgata-ground entered, the four wisdoms perfected and bright.
- **The sublime awakening / sarvajña (妙覺／薩婆若)** — (classification) / sarvajña (Skt, sarvajña = all-knowledge) — the sublime awakening = ultimate awakening (the buddha-fruit); "the aspect of ignorance's ending is the vajra (the path's last step), the aspect of ending being without aspect is the sarvajña (the ultimate)."
- **The two hindrances (二障)** — kleśa-āvaraṇa / jñeya-āvaraṇa — the affliction-hindrance and the cognitive hindrance; the vajra-like samādhi in a single thought-moment severs both at once and enters the tathāgata-ground.
- **Complete / forming (圓成／漸成)** — (book's reading) — the Tathāgata's ultimate non-abiding nirvāṇa is "complete" (only the Buddha possessing all four); the bodhisattva's non-abiding nirvāṇa is "forming" (a causal-level process); their reconciliation is left to the author.

## VIII. Axes, Seam, Reserved to the Author

- **Realization axis / attainment axis (行軸／證軸)** — (series classification) — among the four axes faith-understanding-realization-attainment, "realization" speaks of "arrival" (the ladder of stages), "attainment" (this axis) of "the mastery after arrival."
- **Twin / two-axis seam (雙生／兩軸接縫)** — (series structure) — this volume is the twin of the realization axis's *The Sublime Awakening* (one moment of the sublime awakening, two faces: realization = the path's last step, attainment = the mastery that need take no step further); the realization and attainment axes end together at the sublime-awakening seam—the second seam (mirroring the first, where *The Four Fruits of the Path* and *The Arhat's Mastery* meet at the exhaustion of the outflows).
- **The "self" of permanence-bliss-self-purity (常樂我淨之「我」)** — (reserved to the author · the book's weightiest) — the line between nirvāṇa's true self and the ordinary being's false self, the middle way of the self / no-self teachings, the universality of the buddha-nature (the icchantika)—the book records the scripture and does not adjudicate, leaving the telling to the author to rule; before that ruling it enters neither chapter title nor part-introduction.
- **Only the Buddha possesses all four (唯佛具四)** — (Ch. · reserved to the author) — the four nirvāṇas a Buddha alone fully possesses (the two vehicles' arhats may have the prior three); its cross-axis reconciliation with the two vehicles' without-remainder and the bodhisattva's causal level is left to the author.
- **Heteromorphic, not hierarchically superior (異質而非高下)** — (the bodhisattva volume's locked line · cross-axis convergence) — the three masteries (arhat, bodhisattva, Tathāgata) stand heteromorphic and side by side, each true; their cross-axis positioning (how the buddha-fruit is positioned against the bodhisattva's causal level and the two vehicles' fruit) is left to the author.
- **Turning-from-the-small-to-the-great / the two vehicles' ultimacy (迴小向大／二乘究竟)** — (Ch. · reserved to the author) — whether and how the two vehicles turn toward the great, and whether the two vehicles' fruit is ultimate; the book does not adjudicate, this belonging to the author's doctrinal discernment.
